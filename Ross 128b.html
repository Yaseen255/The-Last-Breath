Ross 128b

Ross 128b is an Earth-sized exoplanet located approximately 11 light-years away from Earth, orbiting the red dwarf star Ross 128 in the constellation Virgo. Discovered in 2017, it has a mass about 1.35 times that of Earth, making it a potentially rocky planet. Ross 128b orbits its host star very closely, completing one orbit in just 9.9 days. Despite this close proximity, it lies in the star's habitable zone, where conditions might allow liquid water to exist on its surface, depending on the characteristics of its atmosphere.

One of the reasons Ross 128b stands out is the relatively quiet nature of its host star. Unlike many other red dwarfs that emit frequent and intense stellar flares, which can harm planetary atmospheres, Ross 128 is less active. This reduces the likelihood of harmful radiation stripping away any atmosphere Ross 128b may have, increasing its potential to sustain life. However, the actual atmospheric composition and surface conditions of Ross 128b are still unknown, and much more research is needed to determine whether it has an atmosphere capable of regulating temperatures for habitability.

Overall, Ross 128b remains one of the most intriguing exoplanets in the search for Earth-like worlds. Its Earth-like mass, location in the habitable zone, and the benign behavior of its host star make it a promising candidate for future studies. While challenges remain in observing its atmosphere and surface directly, Ross 128b is a top target for researchers hoping to find planets that could potentially harbor life beyond our solar system.
