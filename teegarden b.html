teegarden

Teegarden b is an Earth-sized exoplanet located approximately 12 light-years away in the constellation Aries, orbiting the red dwarf star known as Teegarden's Star. This star is significantly smaller and cooler than our Sun, which allows Teegarden b to reside within the star's habitable zone, where conditions may permit the existence of liquid water on its surface. With an orbital period of just 4.9 days, Teegarden b is much closer to its host star than Earth is to the Sun, which raises intriguing possibilities about its climate and atmospheric conditions.

One of the most compelling aspects of Teegarden b is its mass, which is very close to that of Earth, suggesting it could be a rocky planet with a solid surface. This stability enhances the prospects for habitability, making Teegarden b an exciting candidate in the search for life beyond our solar system.

Discovered in 2019 using the radial velocity method, Teegarden b is part of a two-planet system, the other being Teegarden c, which also lies within the habitable zone but at a greater distance from the star. While much remains unknown about Teegarden b's atmosphere and surface conditions, its proximity to Earth and Earth-like characteristics make it a prime target for future astronomical research. Continued observations could reveal more about its potential to host life and contribute valuable insights into the diversity of planets in our galaxy.
