Ross 128 b

Ross 128 b is an exoplanet located 11 light-years from Earth, orbiting the cool red dwarf star Ross 128 in the constellation Virgo. It lies within the habitable zone, making it a candidate for supporting liquid water and potentially life. About 1.4 times the size of Earth, Ross 128 b completes an orbit in 9.9 days and receives a similar amount of energy from its star as Earth does from the Sun. Discovered in 2017, it has become a key target for research into extraterrestrial life.

Story

This project features an educational story set in 2186, when Earth's resources are nearly depleted, prompting humanity to seek life beyond the solar system. Aimed at middle and high school students, it follows Pluto, a determined high school student selected to join a team exploring exoplanets. As Pluto and his team embark on their adventure, they face challenges and learn about the scientific principles of exoplanet discovery, including factors that determine habitability. The narrative intertwines thrilling sci-fi elements with scientific facts, making complex concepts accessible and exciting. The project aims to spark curiosity and a love for science, emphasizing the importance of exploration in understanding the universe. Ultimately, it inspires students to dream big and consider the possibilities of life beyond Earth.
